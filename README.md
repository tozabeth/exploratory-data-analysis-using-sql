# Exploratory Data Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview
This project performs exploratory data analysis (EDA) on a sales dataset using SQL.  
The goal is to uncover actionable insights about **customers, products, and revenue trends** to support data-driven business decisions.

The analysis uses **three main tables**: `Products`, `Customers`, and `Sales`.

---

## Dataset Description

### Tables

- **Products (`dim_products`)**
  - Fields: `product_id`, `category`, `subcategory`, `product_name`, `cost`, `price`  
- **Customers (`dim_customers`)**
  - Fields: `customer_id`, `first_name`, `last_name`, `gender`, `country`, `birthdate`  
- **Sales (`fact_sales`)**
  - Fields: `order_number`, `customer_key`, `product_key`, `order_date`, `quantity`, `sales_amount`

These tables are joined using **primary/foreign keys** (`customer_key` and `product_key`) to generate metrics and reports.

---

## Analysis Sections

### 1. Table Exploration
- Inspecting all products, customers, and sales data  
- Identifying distinct categories, subcategories, and countries  
- Determining first and last order dates, customer ages, and total sales years  

### 2. Key Metrics Calculation
- **Total Sales**  
- **Average Selling Price**  
- **Total Orders**  
- **Total Products and Customers**  
- **Number of Customers Who Placed Orders**  

These metrics provide a high-level overview of business performance.

### 3. Category, Customer, and Country Analysis
- Total customers by country and gender  
- Total products by category  
- Total revenue generated by each customer  
- Total revenue and average cost per category  

### 4. Top/Bottom Performers
- Top 5 and bottom 5 products by revenue  
- Top 10 customers by revenue  
- Ranking using **window functions (`ROW_NUMBER()`)** to identify leaders and laggards  

### 5. Trend Analysis Over Time
- Total sales, revenue, and customer activity per year  
- Monthly trends to identify seasonality and peaks in demand  

---

## SQL Techniques Used
- **Joins**: INNER JOIN, LEFT JOIN to combine multiple tables  
- **Aggregations**: SUM, AVG, COUNT to calculate totals and averages  
- **Window Functions**: ROW_NUMBER() for ranking products and customers  
- **Filtering & Grouping**: DISTINCT, GROUP BY, WHERE clauses  
- **Time-based Analysis**: YEAR(), MONTH(), DATEDIFF(), DATETRUNC()  

---

## Key Insights (Examples)
- The **top 5 products** generate a significant portion of total revenue  
- **Customer distribution** varies by country and gender  
- Categories with higher average costs may indicate higher-profit margins  
- **Sales trends** show seasonal peaks, useful for inventory and marketing planning
